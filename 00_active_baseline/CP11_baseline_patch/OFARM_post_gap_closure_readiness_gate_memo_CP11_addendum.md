## CP11 Sustainable Autonomous Farming Charter readiness addendum

CP11 improves the active baseline by adding a constitutional governance layer for sustainability-sensitive recommendations, plans, outputs, claims, exceptions, breaches, and future autonomous-operation hooks.

CP11 is a model/runtime governance closure, not production sustainability automation.

The correct post-CP11 posture remains:

```text
implementation-directed with bounded debt
not production-ready
not autonomous
not robot-ready
not certification-ready
not external-standard-ready
```

## Claims allowed after CP11 acceptance

After CP11 is accepted and reconciled, OFARM may claim:

- bounded model-law support for executable sustainable autonomous farming governance;
- explicit charter rule-class support for constraints, objectives, evidence obligations, approval gates, claim basis, exceptions, and breaches;
- explicit baseline hooks for CP12, CP13, CP14, and CP15;
- controlled alignment of sustainability-sensitive outputs with truth, evidence, current-state, authority, pack, Advisory/Compliance, query, and output law.

## Claims still blocked

OFARM must not claim:

- production sustainability-governance readiness;
- autonomous sustainability decisioning;
- autonomous compliance decisioning;
- robot or machine execution readiness;
- CP12 cyber-physical mission readiness;
- CP13 learning/farm-memory readiness;
- CP14 farm-to-farm intelligence readiness;
- CP15 generated-software delivery readiness;
- legal advice;
- certification readiness;
- live external-standard readiness;
- live farmer-pilot validation;
- natural-capital accounting accuracy beyond declared evidence/method/uncertainty basis;
- sustainability claims without SustainabilityClaimBasis and applicable output qualification.

## Evidence required before stronger claims

- CP11 schemas promoted from draft/non-default to current/default;
- P0 conformance fixtures passing;
- runtime policy logs showing charter gate execution;
- trace retrieval for SustainabilityPolicyEvaluationTrace;
- claim-output tests proving SustainabilityClaimBasis enforcement;
- pack-merge conflict tests for sustainability surfaces;
- agent-run tests proving agents cannot approve exceptions or attest claims by default;
- farmer-facing comprehension and burden validation;
- external profile review where sustainability standards, buyer programmes, certification schemes, or regulatory thresholds are referenced.
