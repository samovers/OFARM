## CP11 Sustainable Autonomous Farming Charter hostile-review closure

Phase 6 recommendation was `ACCEPT WITH CHANGES`. Phase 6.1 remediated the blocking defects by adding conditional approval enforcement, non-bypass clauses, evidence/freshness separation, expiry-strict exceptions, stronger claim-basis requirements, output-qualification contradiction checks, data-sharing guardrails, external scheme role classification, and draft/non-default currentness staging.

## Closed or substantially reduced

- sustainability is now executable governance, not aspirational prose;
- hard constraints are distinct from optimisation objectives;
- objective priority and trade-off posture are explicit;
- sustainability evidence source, quality, and freshness are separated;
- sustainability claims require explicit claim basis;
- charter exceptions require scope, approval, evidence, and expiry;
- charter breaches are governed records and do not automatically become Compliance Twin facts;
- agents cannot approve high-governance charter actions by default;
- robot/machine execution remains deferred to CP12;
- learning/farm-memory remains deferred to CP13;
- farm-to-farm intelligence remains deferred to CP14;
- generated-software delivery remains deferred to CP15.

## Still open

- production runtime evidence;
- live farmer-pilot validation;
- full sustainability metric-profile catalogue;
- external sustainability scheme/certification review;
- natural-capital accounting methodology;
- robot/machine mission law;
- controlled experimentation and farm-memory promotion law;
- farm-to-farm intelligence and federated learning law;
- generated-software delivery governance;
- full conformance-runner integration beyond P0 fixture set.

## Hostile-reader verdict

CP11 is acceptable as a controlled constitutional extension once the architect accepts it and the currentness maps are updated. It should not be used as a claim of production sustainability automation, certification readiness, autonomous compliance, robot execution, or external-standard readiness.
