# Multiparty farm networks and sister-platform boundaries — supporting research synthesis v0.1

> Source note (2026-04-21): This note adapts the performed Deep Research report on real-world multiparty farm networks, cooperative dynamics, advisory relations, mutual aid, community signaling, and sister-platform boundary design. Inline web citation markers were removed for repository portability. Treat this as supporting research and re-verify external claims before promoting any recommendation into active law.

## Executive summary

The research result is directionally clear.
OFARM’s active core was already strong on:
- explicit authority
- explicit sharing
- revocation
- twin separation
- currentness/freshness discipline
- output traceability

The main unresolved architectural stress was not “multi-user support” in general.
It was the lack of durable relationship semantics and the lack of an explicit OFARM-side picture for adjacent sister platforms.

The strongest field-facing conclusions were:
- small farms often operate with low administrative capacity, overlapping family labour, and occasional helpers
- larger farms behave more like managed organisations with role separation, hired labour, and contractors
- owner, operator, tenant, and landlord are often different parties
- cooperatives are function-specific and do not automatically become blanket farm operators
- contractors, custom operators, and neighbour-help arrangements are normal rather than exotic
- advisory trust is common, but trusted advisors should not silently gain signatory authority
- social/community channels are operationally useful but epistemically noisy
- marketplace/trading flows revolve around contracts, delivery windows, grades, and disputes rather than simple “sell produce” events

## Architectural implication for OFARM

The research pointed to four main conclusions for OFARM:

### 1. Preserve the current OFARM spine
OFARM should keep its existing constitutional spine:
- assertion/history-first authority
- governed current-state materialization
- explicit authority/delegation/sharing separation
- purpose-shaped compiled outputs
- explicit review and promotion law

The report did **not** justify reopening that baseline.

### 2. Add durable relationship memory, not a new permission system
The real-world dynamics repeatedly depended on durable relationships such as:
- beneficial ownership
- tenancy / lease
- operational stewardship
- cooperative membership / administration
- advisor-of-record
- employment / contracted service engagement
- pooled responsibility
- contract-linked buyer relation

These relationships should inform authority, sharing, publication, and audit.
They should not silently replace those systems.

### 3. Keep trading outside core OFARM truth
Trading/marketplace workflows are real and important.
They also form a different truth family:
- offers
- inquiries
- orders
- negotiated contracts
- delivery windows
- commercial disputes
- settlement summaries
- payment references

The research therefore supported a sister-platform stance rather than turning OFARM into order management, payment execution, or hidden ERP.

### 4. Establish OFARM Social at the boundary, not as farm truth
Community and social-network behavior is useful for alerts, discussion, peer help, and local context.
It is also too moderation-sensitive and too easy to misuse as implicit evidence.

The right posture is:
- social signals may suggest
- OFARM still decides what becomes observation, evidence, review, or accepted truth

## Current package consequence

The current package already answered the first half of this research by promoting:
- durable relationship semantics into the accepted RFC lane
- starter exchange-boundary contracts into the accepted RFC lane
- hostile multiparty fixtures into the implementation/conformance lane

The next small controlled step was therefore not a new rewrite.
It was to make the sister-platform picture explicit in package-visible form.

## What this research recommends next

1. Keep OFARM-side sister-platform boundary law inside OFARM itself.
2. Publish a reader-facing linked-domain family map.
3. Publish constitutional tenets for the first sister-platform candidates.
4. Treat full sister-platform constitutions as trigger-based follow-on work, not immediate package law.
5. Keep social boundary starter contracts trigger-based until a real implementation or integration requires them.

## First-priority sister-platform order

The research supports this order:
1. **OFARM Exchange** — first deeper sister-platform lane because contract, delivery, and dispute flows create the sharpest risk of hidden truth drift.
2. **OFARM Social** — establish now at the tenets level so the boundary is explicit, but defer deeper law until a real moderation/community implementation trigger exists.
