# OFARM Sustainable Autonomous Farming Charter and Objective Policy v0.1

Status: CP11 companion artifact candidate  
Authority: active normative support only after acceptance; does not override baseline or accepted RFC law

## Purpose

This companion explains how CP11 should be interpreted in implementation, conformance, and product-facing contexts.

## Interpretation rules

1. Sustainability is executable governance, not marketing.
2. Hard constraints outrank optimisation objectives.
3. Optimisation objectives do not create authority.
4. Evidence source, evidence quality, and freshness are distinct.
5. Sustainability claims require claim basis.
6. Advisory sustainability material remains Advisory Twin unless bridged through gates.
7. Charter exceptions are scoped, approved, evidence-linked, and expiry-aware.
8. Charter breaches are governed records, not automatic legal/compliance facts.
9. External schemes are anchors/profiles/mappings/evidence/attestation wrappers, not hidden OFARM law.
10. CP11 does not authorise robot/machine execution, experimentation, farm-to-farm intelligence, or generated software deployment.

## Example hard constraints

- no operation inside protected habitat zone without explicit allowed context;
- no claim-bearing output from stale soil/water/materialisation basis;
- no chemical/input recommendation where active prohibition applies;
- no partner disclosure without sharing grant or public-authority basis;
- no open-ended charter exception.

## Example optimisation objectives

- reduce nitrogen-loss risk;
- improve water-use efficiency;
- maintain yield stability;
- reduce pesticide-risk load;
- improve soil-health indicator trend;
- reduce energy/emissions intensity.

## Example prohibited trade-offs

- yield gain in exchange for violating a non-tradeable water-contamination threshold;
- short-term margin gain in exchange for hidden biodiversity-habitat impact;
- claim-readiness in exchange for weak or stale evidence;
- partner disclosure in exchange for buyer programme pressure without explicit grant.

## Example review-required trade-offs

- emergency intervention with partial evidence;
- temporary deviation from normal input-governance threshold;
- certification-profile conflict requiring objective-priority resolution;
- public sustainability output based on modelled rather than measured values.

## Display guidance

Farmer-facing surfaces should distinguish:

```text
advisory-only
claim-blocking
claim-ready
review-required
exception-requested
exception-approved
breach-suspected
breach-confirmed
breach-contested
```

The UI may simplify but must not hide material limitations, stale-basis posture, weak evidence, prohibited uses, or authority requirements.
