
# OFARM Farm-to-Farm Intelligence Boundary Policy v0.1

Status: CP14 companion policy candidate  
Authority: active companion artifact if accepted; subordinate to baseline and accepted RFCs  
Amendment: CP14 — Farm-to-Farm Intelligence Boundary

## Purpose

This note interprets CP14 for implementation and review. It does not override baseline law or the CP14 RFC.

## Core policy posture

```text
Cross-farm intelligence is advisory by default.
Farm-to-farm sharing is not authority.
Aggregation is not anonymisation by assertion.
Regional alerts are not farm-level truth.
Benchmark deltas are not compliance facts.
Federated-learning contribution is not model deployment authority.
CP13 local learning may not cross farm boundaries without CP14 governance.
```

## Practical interpretation

- A regional alert may suggest scouting or review, but does not assert that a pest/disease is present on the local farm.
- A benchmark delta may inform advisory analysis, but does not create compliance status, certification status, ranking law, or buyer entitlement.
- A CP13 farm-memory or seasonal-learning artifact remains local unless packaged through CP14 and qualified for cross-farm use.
- A federated-learning contribution is a governed contribution boundary, not model deployment approval.
- Aggregation and deidentification need evidence and risk posture; anonymisation is a stronger claim and requires stronger basis.
- Revocation must propagate to known grants, packages, outputs, receipts, and training-use records where policy requires it.

## Implementation warning

Do not build CP14 as a social network, marketplace, public benchmark product, reputation graph, or production federated-learning platform. Those require separate law or sister-platform constitutions.
