# OFARM Exchange constitutional tenets v0.1

Date: 2026-04-21  
Status: active supporting context artifact  
Scope: establish the minimum high-level tenets for the first OFARM commerce/contract sister-platform lane without freezing detailed implementation law too early

---

## 1. Status and intent

This document is intentionally a **tenets** note, not a full constitution.
Its job is to make OFARM Exchange legible in relation to OFARM core.

---

## 2. Core tenets

### Tenet 1 — OFARM Exchange owns commercial process truth
OFARM Exchange is the home for commercial process truth such as:
- offers
- inquiries
- counteroffers
- orders
- negotiated contract state
- delivery appointments and commercial dispute context
- settlement summaries and payment references

### Tenet 2 — OFARM core retains governed farm truth
OFARM Exchange does not replace OFARM for:
- farm-scoped operational truth
- evidence and review lineage
- current-state materialization
- authority, delegation, sharing, or revocation
- governed publication basis and compiled outputs

### Tenet 3 — Contract existence does not create OFARM access
A contract, order, or buyer relationship inside OFARM Exchange does not by itself create read access to OFARM artifacts.
Access still depends on OFARM-side sharing/publication law.

### Tenet 4 — Exchange may reference OFARM outputs; it may not silently rewrite them
OFARM Exchange may request or carry:
- PassportView outputs
- DocumentAssembly outputs
- `ContractReference`
- `CrossPlatformGrantReceipt`
- other explicit OFARM boundary artifacts

It may not silently widen, mutate, or relabel OFARM truth by doing so.

### Tenet 5 — Commercial acceptance does not equal agronomic or compliance truth
Order acceptance, delivery acknowledgment, buyer complaint, settlement summary, or payment success must not silently become:
- accepted execution truth
- lot identity change
- compliance success/failure
- OFARM review supersession

### Tenet 6 — Payment execution remains outside OFARM core
Payment execution and regulated money movement should remain with appropriate third-party financial-service rails.
OFARM Exchange may coordinate references to those flows, but it should not collapse OFARM core into a payments platform.

### Tenet 7 — Disputes return to explicit review paths
Commercial disputes may cause OFARM Exchange to request deeper evidence, additional publication, or a challenge to existing OFARM outputs.
If OFARM truth must change, the change must occur through explicit OFARM review/governance lineage.

### Tenet 8 — Counterparty identity and purpose must stay explicit
OFARM Exchange should preserve who the counterparties are, why OFARM-derived access is being used, and which contract or purpose justifies that access.

### Tenet 9 — OFARM Exchange must not become a hidden OFARM truth mirror
Exchange may cache or retain commercial history and references.
It must not become the place where users actually trust the farm truth “because that is where the deal lives”.

### Tenet 10 — Deeper constitution only on real trigger
A deeper OFARM Exchange constitution becomes justified only when a real prototype, pilot, or implementation charter exists.
Until then, OFARM-side boundary law plus these tenets are enough.

---

## 3. Minimum boundary expectation

At minimum, OFARM Exchange should be designed assuming:
- OFARM grants/publications stay authoritative for OFARM-side access
- OFARM outputs remain purpose-shaped and recipient-shaped
- exchange-owned commercial state stays outside OFARM core truth
- payment references stay references unless a later platform explicitly owns more
