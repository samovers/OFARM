# OFARM Social constitutional tenets v0.1

Date: 2026-04-21  
Status: active supporting context artifact  
Scope: establish the minimum high-level tenets for the first community/social sister-platform lane without freezing moderation, community-governance, or product-law specifics too early

---

## 1. Status and intent

This document is intentionally a **tenets** note, not a full constitution.
Its job is to make OFARM Social explicit as a family member and to prevent social/community behavior from remaining architecturally implied but undefined.

---

## 2. Core tenets

### Tenet 1 — OFARM Social owns conversation and community state
OFARM Social is the home for community-level state such as:
- message threads
- groups and community spaces
- moderation records
- peer recommendations and warnings
- social graph or participation state
- reputation-like or activity-based signals if such features exist

### Tenet 2 — OFARM core retains farm truth
OFARM Social does not replace OFARM for:
- formal observations
- evidence records
- review decisions
- current-state materialization
- compliance truth
- governed compiled outputs

### Tenet 3 — Social signals may suggest; OFARM decides
A post, warning, rumour, reputation signal, or repeated community message may trigger observation or follow-up work.
It must not silently become accepted OFARM truth.

### Tenet 4 — Popularity is not evidence sufficiency
Likes, forwards, shares, repetition, moderator prestige, or group status must not substitute for OFARM evidence quality, review, or promotion law.

### Tenet 5 — Provenance must stay visible when signals cross the boundary
If OFARM Social sends anything OFARM-relevant across the boundary, the ingress path should preserve at least:
- source identity or source handle posture
- time
- provenance reference
- moderation state where relevant
- confidence or uncertainty posture if expressed
- who promoted or ingested the signal into OFARM-follow-up work

### Tenet 6 — Group membership does not equal blanket farm access
A community, cooperative discussion group, or peer network inside OFARM Social does not by itself create OFARM authority or farm read access.
OFARM-side sharing still governs OFARM artifacts.

### Tenet 7 — Social and advisory proximity should remain bounded
OFARM Social may sit close to advisory communities, local-warning groups, or peer-learning behavior.
That does not make it the Advisory Twin.
The OFARM Advisory Twin remains inside OFARM’s governed model/runtime boundary.

### Tenet 8 — Deeper constitution only on real trigger
A deeper OFARM Social constitution becomes justified only when a real community/moderation implementation trigger exists.
Until then, OFARM-side boundary law plus these tenets are enough.

---

## 3. Minimum boundary expectation

At minimum, OFARM Social should be designed assuming:
- social/community state stays outside OFARM core truth
- OFARM-facing signal ingress remains explicit and review-aware
- OFARM outputs shared into social/community contexts stay bounded and purpose-shaped
- OFARM Social does not become a shortcut around OFARM evidence, review, or sharing law
