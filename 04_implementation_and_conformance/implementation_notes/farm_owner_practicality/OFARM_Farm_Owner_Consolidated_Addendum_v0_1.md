## What is already useful

The thread produced usable material in four categories.

### 1. Ordinary-farm executable rehearsal

The package now has rehearsal coverage for offline contractor late sync, grain/storage lot disputes, post-filing late evidence, partial failure and duplicate sync, delayed lab/nutrient recalculation, and vendor exit.

These prove OFARM can express the scenarios under controlled conditions. They do not prove OFARM is usable on farms.

### 2. Live-pilot intake apparatus

The package now has intake, redaction, provenance, metric, and reviewer-disposition protocols for real farm evidence.

These prepare the evidence lane. They do not create live evidence.

### 3. FMIS shadow import and weak-record handling

- weak document refs remain `EvidenceReference`;

- weak driver/material-transport hints remain `UnresolvedActorIdentity`;
- missing review/promotion creates review gaps and loss-map items;
- current-state FMIS records do not become OFARM governed truth.

## What is not proven

## Phase 9 trigger

Phase 9 is blocked until at least one of the following occurs:

1. source-owner evidence closes one or more FMIS gaps;
2. a second or third real redacted FMIS case confirms or breaks the adapter assumptions;
3. an OFARM-compatible runtime captures a real farm workflow live.

Only then should the project decide whether to keep changes as implementation/conformance, harden draft contracts, create RFC extensions, or patch baseline law.
