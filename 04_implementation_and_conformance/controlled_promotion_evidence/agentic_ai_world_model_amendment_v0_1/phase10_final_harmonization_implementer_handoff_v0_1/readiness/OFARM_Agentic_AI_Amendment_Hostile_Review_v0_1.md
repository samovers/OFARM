# OFARM Agentic AI Amendment Hostile Review v0.1

## Hostile verdict

The amendment is strong enough for pre-implementation handoff, but it would be unsafe to market it as runtime-ready.

## What the amendment gets right

It preserves OFARM's central architecture: assertion/history-first truth, governed materialization, two logical twins, explicit authority, explicit evidence, promotion law, pack law, query discipline, output taxonomy, and data sovereignty.

It refuses the main agentic failure modes:

- generic `AgentOutput` truth bucket;
- third AI twin;
- hidden agent memory as truth;
- tool-call success as governance success;
- world-model state as current state;
- handoff-carried authority;
- manifest self-description as authority;
- self-review or self-promotion;
- cross-farm learning by default.

## What remains vulnerable

The remaining vulnerabilities are implementation and promotion vulnerabilities:

1. Candidate schemas can drift if promoted piecemeal without crosswalking fields.
2. App builders may still collapse draft/advisory/current/frozen states in UI.
3. Runtime logs may be incomplete unless trace retrieval is made mandatory.
4. Sharing/revocation semantics need executable tests.
5. World-model calibration and invalidation need runtime evidence.
6. Two-agent compatibility remains unproven.
7. Minimum capture and formula/calculation materials remain deliberately held.

## Hard no-go before runtime claim

No runtime claim should be made until the Phase 9 hostile suite executes and passes with expected blocked-action traces.

## Final hostile conclusion

The amendment is architecturally conservative and appropriately bounded. The next risk is not missing architecture; it is premature readiness claims.
