# CP9 disposition memo

Disposition: **keep as implementation/conformance evidence**.

CP9 validates fixture-level farmer-facing display and workflow invariants. It is intentionally not a law-promotion phase. The correct authority posture is to use CP9 as evidence for follow-on UX hardening and CP10 claim-limit updates, not as proof of production or live pilot readiness.

## Why this is not active law

- Synthetic scenario fixtures are not field evidence.
- UX checks can reveal design failures, but they do not establish live comprehension, burden tolerance, or adoption.
- Active CP1 through CP8 law and contracts already define the governance boundaries; CP9 tests whether farmer-facing surfaces preserve those boundaries.

## Required interpretation

A passing CP9 runner means: selected synthetic farmer-value scenarios preserved OFARM qualification, request, sharing, offline, world-model, handoff, and dispute-reconstruction invariants.

It does not mean: farmers have validated the UI, burden thresholds are calibrated, production deployment is ready, or OFARM can make stronger public readiness claims.
