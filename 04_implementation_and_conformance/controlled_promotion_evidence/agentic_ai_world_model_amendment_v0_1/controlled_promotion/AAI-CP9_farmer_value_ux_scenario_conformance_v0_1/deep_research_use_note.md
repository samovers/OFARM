# CP9 Deep Research use note

Source: `source_inputs/deep-research-report-30.md`.

CP9 applies the report's farmer-facing UX guidance as fixture-level checks:

- display qualification before content;
- keep advisory/compliance scope, freshness, evidence, permission, approval, and last-human-verified status visible;
- route low-urgency requests through daily briefs rather than interruptive prompts;
- show burden, relevance window, why-it-matters text, alternatives, and completion criteria;
- label offline captures as captured locally, not accepted;
- require fresh policy checks before sharing after sync;
- prevent summary leaks after detail redaction;
- keep world-model scenarios visibly Advisory Twin-only;
- provide time-ordered dispute reconstruction.

The report also says farmer-facing workflow conformance should remain experiment-first until live evidence exists. CP9 therefore does not make readiness claims.
