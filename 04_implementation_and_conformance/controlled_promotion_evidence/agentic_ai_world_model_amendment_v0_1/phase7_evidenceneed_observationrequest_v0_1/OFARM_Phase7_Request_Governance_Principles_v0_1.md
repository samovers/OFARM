# OFARM Phase 7 — Request Governance Principles v0.1

## Principles

1. **Request is not truth.** A request indicates missing or desired information. It does not assert that the requested fact exists.
2. **Request is not evidence.** Captured artifacts may become evidence candidates; the request itself is only a need/task artifact.
3. **Request is not promotion.** Satisfying a request may unblock review or promotion, but does not itself promote anything.
4. **Request basis must be explicit.** Every request must identify the workflow, rule, scenario, output, promotion path, or review question that generated it.
5. **Farmer burden must be visible.** Priority and burden estimate are required for generated requests.
6. **Noise must be controlled.** Duplicate, stale, low-value, or conflicting requests must be suppressed, collapsed, or clearly presented.
7. **Compliance blocking requires authority.** No AI, world model, or app surface may invent a compliance blocker without active OFARM basis.
8. **Local knowledge may respond.** Farmers may satisfy, annotate, contest, or contextualize requests with local knowledge, subject to normal governance.
