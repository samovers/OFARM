# OFARM External Code Binding Currentness Deep Research Prompt v0.1

Use this prompt before writing the Phase 4 external registry/currentness amendment.

```text
You are conducting Deep Research for OFARM 2, a crop-farming semantic reference model and platform architecture.

Your task is to provide current, source-grounded research for a narrow OFARM external code-binding and registry-currentness profile.

Do not rewrite OFARM. Do not propose a new truth model. Treat the active OFARM baseline as governing. External standards and registries may act only as semantic anchors, code bindings, exchange mappings, runtime surfaces, or attestation wrappers. They must not become hidden OFARM law.

RESEARCH_SCOPE
Project: OFARM 2
Target amendment: External code-binding currentness and one narrow crop-protection profile
Preferred profile: crop-protection product authorization / product identity for one EU jurisdiction, preferably Slovenia if official sources are available and verifiable. If Slovenia cannot be researched reliably from official sources, select another EU jurisdiction with clearer official registry access and explain why.
Use date: {insert current date}
Output purpose: support an OFARM amendment for ReferenceSnapshot, AgronomicIdentityBinding, AgronomicCodeBindingProfile, ExternalRegistryVerificationTrace, and high-consequence PassportView refusal/review rules.

OFARM CONTEXT
OFARM already has assertion/history-first truth, governed current-state materialization, separate recommendation/prescription/plan/execution/as-applied/accepted consequence/correction/dispute, AgronomicIdentityBinding, AgronomicCodeBindingProfile, ReferenceSnapshot, AgronomicReconstructionPolicy, AgronomicReconstructionTrace, SemanticPathAlias governance, and PassportView/DocumentAssembly separation.

RESEARCH QUESTIONS

1. Identify the official crop-protection product authorization source for the selected jurisdiction. Determine responsible authority, jurisdiction, access method, currentness/version visibility, identifier stability, product-name sufficiency, represented authorization details, update cadence, limitations, and official citations.
2. Identify EU-level active-substance/pesticide database context. Determine suitable OFARM role, what it can identify, whether it can replace jurisdictional product authorization, limitations, and citations.
3. Research supporting schemes: EPPO, BBCH, UCUM, QUDT, CPVO Variety Finder/UPOV PLUTO, OECD Seed Schemes, GS1 GTIN/Digital Link, AGROVOC, and Crop Ontology. For each, give owner, role, identifier pattern, currentness/versioning, access/licensing limitations, suitable use, and citations.
4. Recommend minimum ReferenceSnapshot and ExternalRegistryVerificationTrace fields for high-consequence crop-protection binding.
5. Recommend fail-closed / require-review rules for free-text product only, GTIN only, multiple matches, expired/withdrawn authorization, active substance without product authorization, stale source, unavailable registry, wrong jurisdiction, missing snapshot, and prescription/as-applied mismatch.
6. Produce a narrow AgronomicCodeBindingProfile blueprint with scope, standard role map, binding requirements, unresolved behavior, snapshots, verification trace behavior, quantity/unit policy, evidence floor, pack-merge posture, PassportView rules, DocumentAssembly annex rules, limitations, and non-claims.

Rules: use official or primary sources wherever possible; cite every factual claim; distinguish law/regulation, registry data, vocabulary, exchange format, commercial identifier, and advisory source; do not invent identifiers, URLs, dates, or registry behavior; do not provide legal advice; flag uncertainty; do not claim OFARM is externally standard-ready.
```
